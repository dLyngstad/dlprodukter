Why Gesture?

Gesticulation isn't just "hand-waving." It provides a spatial scaffold for abstract thoughts. By using your hands, you reduce the cognitive load on your audience, allowing them to "see" the math or physics in the air.

"If you can't move it, you don't understand it."

SCIENCE IN MOTION

Mastering Gesticulation to Explain the Invisible

1. Scaling the Universe

When explaining size (from atoms to galaxies), use the "Z-Axis Pull." Start with hands close together at eye level, then pull them apart while stepping backward. This physical expansion anchors the concept of "exponential growth" in the viewer's mind.

2. The Molecular Dance

Explain Entropy by using sharp, chaotic finger movements versus slow, fluid arm movements for Laminar Flow. Mimicking the physical state of matter makes thermodynamics visceral rather than just mathematical.

3. Invisible Forces

Use Resistance Gestures to explain Magnetic Fields or Gravity. Tension in your muscles while "pulling" an invisible object helps the audience "feel" the force acting upon the system.
