Gestikulering

A Catalog of Didactic Hand Modelling in Science.

Physics

The Right-Hand Rule

The Model: Point the thumb in the direction of the current. Let the fingers curl inward.

The Concept: Shows the direction of the circular magnetic field around a wire.

Physics

Wave Types

The Model: Snake-like up/down motion (Transverse) vs. pulsing push/pull motion (Longitudinal).

The Concept: Medium movement relative to wave direction (e.g., light vs. sound).

Physics

Particle States

The Model: Fists vibrating together (solid), sliding over each other (liquid), bouncing far apart (gas).

The Concept: The kinetic theory of matter.

Chemistry

Chirality

The Model: Palms up, showing they are mirror images but cannot be perfectly superimposed.

The Concept: Enantiomers and "handedness" in molecular structures.

Chemistry

Enzyme "Lock & Key"

The Model: Hand in a rigid "C" shape docking with a fist from the other hand.

The Concept: Enzyme-substrate specificity.

Biology

Sliding Filaments

The Model: Point fingers of both hands together, then slide them deeply between one another.

The Concept: Actin and myosin sliding during muscle contraction.

Biology

Phagocytosis

The Model: A "pac-man" hand wrapping around a small object and pulling inward.

The Concept: Cell membrane engulfing large particles.

Earth Science

Tectonic Plates

The Model: Flat hands sliding apart (Divergent), pushing to buckle (Convergent), or rubbing edges (Transform).

The Concept: Plate boundaries and resulting geography.
